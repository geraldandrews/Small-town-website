nearby

attractions

great scenery

Rocky Gap offers some of the best country mountain scenery in the Appalachian Mountains for those who enjoy the outdoors. The drive up East River Mountain on Route 52 from Rocky Gap to the overlook on top of it in neighboring Bluefield WV is sure to please anytime of the year.

outdoors/fishing

Some of the best known trout fishing spots can be found along the town's creeks. The abundance and size of trout in the creeks, make the spots some of the most popular for area fishermen. Nearby Jefferson and George Washington National Forests in Bland County VA are known for some of the best fishing, camping, and trails, including the Appalachian Trail.

shopping

Most of the shopping centers can be found over the mountain along Route 460 in neighboring Bluefield WV and Bluefield VA. The Walmart and Lowes plazas can be found going west into the Virginia side and the Mercer Mall can be found going the east direction into the West Virginia side.

historical attractions

Wolfe Creek Indian Village and Museum is one of many nearby attractions in the area. It is a historical site, which provides a glimpse of the way of life of the state and area's first Native American settlers. The village and museum has attracted visitors from all over the world and the United States.

about

Rocky Gap is an unincorporated small town located in Bland County Virginia located in the south western part of the state. As of 2016, it is estimated that a total of 511 people live in Rocky Gap. The town is connected to neighboring Bluefield, WV by the East River Mountain Tunnel, which takes interstate 77 beneath East River Mountain. Rocky Gap is also known by area fishermen for it's abundance of trout fishing in it's creeks and by many others for it's great small town country scenery. The town is also now home of the Bland County High School Bears, the county's only high school.

history

The old bank building of Rocky Gap was built a few years before the Great Depression. Later on it became a local bakery and then an antique store. The building is now vacant, but was recently renovated. Over the years, the town has also been home to many small convenience stores that have come and gone. Most notable is Carroll Brothers grocery, which was open for many years before closing in the late 1980's and a restaurant next door called the Eagle's Nest and then Woody's that eventually closed in the 1990's. Both buildings were eventually demolished, changing the appearance of that section of the town. While very few businesses still remain and the town awaits a regrowth, it is still a peaceful and beautiful small town rich with the great memories from years past by it's great people.
